Honest answers

The questions you're already thinking.

Is this SaaS? Does our data touch your servers?

No, and never. Conduit is self-hosted software — you run the containers in your own cloud account. We are not in the request path, we never see your prompts, code, or keys, and there's no telemetry or license callback. The subscription buys the license, updates, and support — not hosting.

Couldn't we just self-host an open-source gateway like LiteLLM?

You can — if your security team will accept unsupported open source on your AI egress path, and you have a platform team to harden, patch, and document it. Conduit is for orgs where that's not true: a supported, compliance-documented control plane with signed releases, a patching commitment, and someone accountable — plus the governance feedback loop (alert → promote-to-block) built for coding-agent traffic.

Does it change model quality or break streaming?

No. Conduit is a transparent proxy: request bodies aren't modified, streaming (SSE) passes through untouched with metering off the hot path, and a disallowed model is a clear 403 — never a silent downgrade. Overhead is under 5ms.
